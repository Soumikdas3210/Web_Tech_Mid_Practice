Boost readiness as earthquake risk remains high

Published: November 30, 2025

Bangladesh faces a high risk of major earthquakes due to its location along three active tectonic plate faults, experts warned yesterday, stressing that preparedness, public awareness and modern technology are crucial to reducing casualties and damage.

The warning came at a seminar titled "Earthquake Awareness, Safety Protocol and Emergency Preparedness", organised by JCX Developments Ltd at a city hotel.

College teacher killed after being run over by motorcycle in Satkhira

Published: November 29, 2025

A college teacher was killed after being hit by a speeding motorcycle in Kadakati area of Ashashuni upazila in Satkhira this afternoon. The deceased was identified as Golam Azam, 45, a teacher of Kadakati Degree College and resident of the area.

Around 2:30pm, he was standing at Kadakati Bazar on the Banka-Kulya road near the school, waiting for a bus to return to his village. At that time, a motorcycle coming from behind hit him at high speed.

Bangladesh moves to import additional 20MW hydropower from Nepal

Published: November 29, 2025

Bangladesh has decided to import an additional 20 megawatts of hydropower from Nepal, expanding the current arrangement under which it buys 40MW for six months each year, between June and November.

"We have proposed to buy an additional 20 megawatts of electricity from Nepal, and they have agreed in principle," Farzana Mamtaz, secretary of the Power Division under the Ministry of Power, Energy and Mineral Resources, told The Daily Star today.
